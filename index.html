A Mediterranean and African fund

Expand the map, Go south to Mediterranean and Africa.

Mediterranean and Africa face common and specific challenges:
Energy challenge: geopolitical turmoil generates a strong need for Europe to access alternative energy provider.
Demographic challenge: the world population growth is focused on Africa (UN). The North Africa subregion demographic forecasts foreseen a growth of 50% between now and 2050, while the northern side of Mediterranean is in a demographic negative trend.
Climate challenge: impendent desertification in the Sahelian region is a major concern to address. The Janus Med Africa Fund target green assets, such as renewable energy, energy storage, new types of batteries and low-carbon transport, and also, economically sustainable reforestation projects.
Structural challenge: to bridge Africa's Infrastructure Gap.
Diplomatic challenge: the bridge or gateway function of the Janus Med Africa Fund is in line with the latest EU projects. By 2050, the United Nations predicts that an additional 2.5 billion people will migrate from rural to urban areas, with nearly 90 percent of this increase concentrated in growth and emerging markets (IMF WP/2022/182).
Laboral challenge and shortage of quality jobs: a deficit of quality jobs in North Africa, the quantitative employment deficit worsened during the last five-year period with significant disparities between regions, particularly affecting women and youth (ADB and WLO).
Growth challenge: The lack of access to finance might largely impact SME’s growth (Nasr and Pierce 2012), and formal SME contribute to the 40% of national income in emerging economies (World Bank 2022).

The investment quality and level across Mediterranean and Africa is still insufficient.

The African continent now has 7 of the 10 most dynamic economies in the world, and this growth is sustained in the longer term by powerful demographic drivers: the population is projected to grow from 1.2 billion to more than 4 billion in 2100 according to UNICEF.
As a result there will be an exponential growth of the basic needs of the population This concerns related to food, urbanization, the needs of a booming middle class, education and the development of health infrastructure.
Cash-like securities and assets under management for infrastructure funds have never been higher, suggesting strong investment activity ahead. Infrastructure investment funds active in Africa have set record fundraising targets for 2021, according to a new analysis by international business law firm Linklaters.
But the current investments in Africa, according to OECD and G20 are still far and behind the European, or Asian ones.

A time of challenges, a time of opportunities.

Mediterranean and Africa face common and specific opportunities:

  • Demographic opportunities,

  • ESG opportunities,

  • Digitalization and Innovation,

  • Growing regional integration,

  • Increase interest for Africa,

  • Alleviate the migratory issue.

JANUS MED AFRICA

Origin. Every meaningful story has a purpose … Our story is not exception

During "The International Conference for Investment and Trade in Libya” of December 2021, SoulVest, together with the conference committee, our international and local partners, have laid the foundations for a new blueprint of international relations and development.
This vision revolutionizes the classic top-down approach in a model that proposes a participatory chain from the international community to the local one.
The structure plays a role that enables African communities to invest in a fund that will attract local and international investors and build a commercial and cultural bridge between Africa, Europe, and the rest of the world.
This bridge will have a financial impact, and foremost a social and environmental impact. By investing in local businesses and communities, it will provide the resources to create new companies and initiatives with a particular regard for ESG performance, which will bring new jobs, enabling the younger generations to stabilize their lives with a renewed clarity and confidence for the future.
This stabilizing function is in line with the neighborhood strategy expressed by the EU and with Mediterranean countries national interests.

Janus Med Africa Fund is an independent private equity fund

The aim of this Fund it is to operate in the Mediterranean African region as a private equity investment vehicle.
Of course, there are challange to overcome as the area still presents some complexity.
Here is the edge we can offer through this Fund: We established a network of close partnerships with local stakeholders. To highlight their commitment, they will be the first corner investors in the structure. In this way we align interest of local actors and communities with the global investors. Their commitment also acts as a guarantee on the bone fide and goodwill for the project’s success.
This convergence, it will lead to setting up different international business and projects that ultimately increase the well-being and live-hood of the Mediterranean region.

ESG by design: SoulVest Impact scoring scheme

ESG factors are at the heart of our projects
Environmental factors:
Carbon neutral impact is one of the metrics we use for improving the environmental performance in our portfolio by optimizing the energy that a company consumes, the waste it generates, the natural resources it uses and the consequences on ecosystems, habitats and of course the inhabitants.
Social factors:
We pay constant attention to social performance which measures a company's commitment to its workers, customers, suppliers and the local community. It covers human rights, diversity and inclusion, health and safety and impact on the community. Damages and corporate reputations as well as legal and regulatory sanctions may also arise in the event of non- compliance with these principles.
Governance factors:
We provide ongoing governance oversight to systematically assess how a company uses policies and controls to inform business decisions, comply with the law, and meet its obligations in fairness to stakeholders.
